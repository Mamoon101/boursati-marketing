# Boursati Launch Posting Plan

This is the practical execution plan for the approved marketing strategy.

Strategy source:

`../../02_marketing_plan/boursati-final-marketing-plan.md`

## Week 1 Goal

Make the account understandable before making it dense.

A viewer should quickly understand:

- Boursati is for Boursa Kuwait.
- It is Arabic-first.
- It helps organize prices, news, disclosures, companies, alerts, summaries, and portfolio review.
- It is available on the App Store.

## Core Message

بورصة الكويت بوضوح، من مكان واحد.

Use this as the spine. Every launch post should support it directly or indirectly.

## Week 1 Posting Logic

After the launch grid, post in this order:

1. Main promise: Boursa Kuwait in one Arabic app.
2. Market clarity: start from a clear summary before details.
3. Disclosure awareness: source-first behavior.
4. Product proof: show the real app after the brand promise lands.
5. Educational carousel: make the account useful, not only promotional.

Use the active paths in:

`../week_1_launch_execution/week-1-posting-order.md`

## Caption Principle

If the visual is abstract, the caption must be practical.

Good launch caption shape:

1. State the product value.
2. Explain why it matters.
3. Close with App Store CTA.

Example:

```text
تابع بورصة الكويت بوضوح.

الأسعار، الأخبار، الإفصاحات، والشركات ضمن تجربة عربية واحدة.

حمّل بورصتي من App Store.
```

## Daily Content Formats

Use these after the launch identity is established:

- Tip of the day: one practical market-following habit.
- Info of the day: one simple market concept.
- Market summary tip: one review prompt for opening, closing, or weekly check-in.
- Product proof: a real screenshot only when it explains a feature.

## Channel Notes

- Instagram feed: use `instagram-feed-profile-grid-safe/` files from the active Week 1 folder.
- Instagram stories: quick prompts and reminders.
- Reels/TikTok: short educational hooks and workflow stories.
- X: concise market-context statements and launch/disclosure messages.

## Do Not Do

- Do not lead with vague poetic lines before explaining the product.
- Do not overuse screenshots.
- Do not post Week 2 or Week 3 until Week 1 is reviewed.
- Do not use investment advice, predictions, buy/sell language, or performance promises.
- Do not mention Android, Google Play, subscriptions, trials, or paid plans.
