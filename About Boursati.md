# About Boursati

This document gives Hermes and any future marketing agent the product context needed before creating strategy, copy, assets, captions, videos, or automation for Boursati.

## Short Version

Boursati helps people in Kuwait follow Boursa Kuwait clearly, in Arabic, from one organized iPhone app.

Boursati is an Arabic-first iPhone app for people who follow the Kuwait stock market. It brings Boursa Kuwait companies, prices, charts, disclosures, PDF filings, financial statements, dividends, portfolio tracking, watchlists, alerts, market summaries, and push notifications into one clean mobile experience.

The app is not a brokerage, not a trading app, and not an investment advisor. It helps users follow the market and understand their own portfolio. It does not let users buy or sell stocks.

Production site: https://boursati.app

## What Boursati Does

Boursati helps Kuwaiti investors answer practical daily questions:

- What is happening in Boursa Kuwait today?
- Which companies are moving?
- Did a company publish a new disclosure?
- Can I open the disclosure PDF quickly on my phone?
- What is in my portfolio?
- How is my portfolio performing?
- Which companies do I want to follow?
- What price alerts do I care about?
- Is there a clear Arabic market summary I can read quickly?

The app organizes this through:

- Home market overview with market status, index movement, gainers, losers, and AI market summary.
- Company pages with prices, chart periods, fundamentals, financial statements, management/governance, disclosures, and PDFs.
- Portfolio tracking with cash balance, positions, transactions, profit/loss, and dividend estimates.
- Watchlist and price alerts.
- Push notifications for disclosures, AI summaries, and price alerts.
- WidgetKit home-screen widgets.
- Arabic-first RTL interface with dark and light modes.

## Who Boursati Serves

Boursati serves Kuwaiti retail investors who follow Boursa Kuwait seriously but do not want the clutter of regional finance apps or the friction of official market websites.

The core user is someone who:

- owns or follows Kuwaiti listed companies,
- checks the market several times a day,
- prefers Arabic for financial context,
- wants disclosures and PDFs without hunting through exchange pages,
- wants portfolio visibility without maintaining everything manually in Excel,
- wants alerts and summaries without hype.

The best mental model is the "morning-coffee investor": someone who opens the app before or during the trading day to understand the market calmly and quickly.

## Positioning

Boursati is the Kuwait-native market follow-up app.

The simple marketing promise is:

> بورصة الكويت بوضوح، من مكان واحد.

English working line:

> Follow Boursa Kuwait clearly, from one organized mobile place.

It sits between:

- the official Boursa Kuwait sources, which are important but not always mobile-friendly,
- regional finance apps, which may be broad but cluttered,
- global apps like Yahoo Finance, which are useful but not Arabic-native or Kuwait-focused,
- brokerage apps, which are for trading rather than calm market follow-up.

Boursati's advantage is focus: it is built around Kuwait, Arabic, disclosures, portfolio visibility, and a serious mobile experience.

## Message Hierarchy

Every campaign should make the product easy to understand before becoming poetic or abstract.

1. First: Boursati helps you follow Boursa Kuwait clearly.
2. Second: It brings prices, news, disclosures, companies, alerts, summaries, and portfolio context into one Arabic app.
3. Third: The strongest behavior is moving from notification to official disclosure quickly.
4. Fourth: The daily habit is starting from the market picture, then going deeper.
5. Fifth: More editorial lines like "كل مسار له مكان" can support the brand after the practical value is clear.

## What Makes It Different

- Kuwait-first: companies, tickers, disclosures, and data flows are centered on Boursa Kuwait.
- Arabic-first: the interface and messaging are written for Arabic users, not translated as an afterthought.
- Disclosure-focused: push notification to source disclosure is one of the key product moments.
- Portfolio-aware: the app helps users follow their own positions and performance.
- Calm finance tone: no hype, no trading pressure, no exaggerated claims.
- Mobile-native: designed as an iOS app, not a web dashboard squeezed into a phone.

## Product Truths Marketing Must Respect

Marketing should be confident, but it must stay accurate.

Do say:

- "تابع السوق بوضوح."
- "إفصاحات الشركات في مكان واحد."
- "محفظتك، أخبارك، وتنبيهاتك ضمن تجربة واحدة."
- "من التنبيه إلى الإفصاح مباشرة."
- "مصمم لمتابعة بورصة الكويت."

Do not say:

- "تداول من التطبيق."
- "اربح أكثر."
- "توصيات شراء وبيع."
- "فرص مضمونة."
- "أسعار لحظية مضمونة."
- "نصائح استثمارية."
- "أفضل تطبيق في الكويت."

Boursati tracks and summarizes market data. It does not predict, recommend, or guarantee investment outcomes.

## Data Sources and Caveats

Boursati collects, cleans, stores, and presents market information from external and internal sources.

Main data sources:

- Boursa Kuwait for company lists, sectors, fundamentals, financial statements, disclosures, and PDF links.
- Yahoo Finance for price and historical chart data.
- Google News RSS for market news.
- Groq for Arabic AI summaries based on collected market data.
- User input for portfolio, watchlist, alerts, and notification preferences.

Important caveats:

- Prices may be delayed.
- Disclosure PDFs depend on Boursa Kuwait source availability.
- Dividend information is useful but can require careful wording because dates, bonus shares, or special cases may vary.
- AI summaries are summaries, not investment advice.
- Portfolio values depend on user-entered positions and available price data.

## Brand Personality

Boursati should feel:

- confident,
- precise,
- calm,
- local,
- modern,
- serious enough for someone's actual portfolio.

It should not feel:

- salesy,
- noisy,
- generic fintech,
- cartoonish,
- speculative,
- overly corporate,
- like a brokerage pushing the user to trade.

The voice should feel like a quiet, competent banker who trusts the reader to understand numbers.

## Visual Direction

The approved marketing direction for this repo is not the older corporate/phone-mockup direction. The current approved system is:

- dark ink backgrounds,
- electric Boursati blue,
- off-white/gray technical line work,
- abstract Kuwait-inspired architectural forms,
- corrected Kuwait Towers line illustration,
- bold Arabic typography,
- editorial grid structure,
- precise CTA alignment,
- selective use of app screenshots only when context requires them.

Screenshots should not be forced into every post. Use app screens only when the post is explaining a real feature or user flow. For broader brand, educational, launch, or market-awareness posts, abstract visual systems are acceptable and often better.

## Core Marketing Themes

Use these themes when creating assets or campaigns:

- Launch awareness: Boursati is now available for Kuwait market followers.
- Market clarity: follow Boursa Kuwait from one organized Arabic app.
- Disclosure speed: from push notification to official disclosure quickly.
- Portfolio calm: track positions without spreadsheet clutter.
- Daily habit: open Boursati with morning coffee before or during the market day.
- Learning content: tip of the day, information of the day, market summary of the day.
- Kuwait-native focus: built around Boursa Kuwait, Arabic users, and local investor behavior.

## Current Business Model

The current App Store build is free. There is no subscription, paywall, restore-purchase flow, or external payment unlock in the accepted build referenced by the product repo.

Do not create marketing copy that mentions subscriptions, free trials, premium tiers, paid plans, or monetization unless the owner explicitly approves a future pricing change.

## Audience-Safe Disclaimer

Use a simple disclaimer when needed:

> بورصتي تطبيق لمتابعة البيانات السوقية فقط، ولا يقدم نصائح أو توصيات استثمارية.

English equivalent:

> Boursati is for market-data follow-up only and does not provide investment advice or recommendations.
